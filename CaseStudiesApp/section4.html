4.1 - Review the following image and provide a diagnosis

What do these radiographs demonstrate?
  • The modified Sharp method (1985), is a radiographic scoring system used in rheumatoid arthritis.
  • Seventeen areas in each hand (marked with circles) are scored (1-5) for erosion and eighteen areas in each hand (marked with parallel lines) are scored (1-4) for joint space narrowing.
  • The radiographs are normal.
  • Additional rheumatoid arthritis scoring methods include: Larsen, van der Heijde modification of Sharp scoring (SvHS), and the Simple Erosion Narrowing Score (SENS)

Reference

  • Ref: Appendix-Imaging Scoring Methods in Arthritis O'Neill, JMD, Essential Imaging in Rheumatology. New York: Springer Science+Business Media; 2015

4.10 - Review the following images and provide a diagnosis

36M with well-controlled rheumatoid arthritis presenting with low back pain with left leg radicular symptoms.
What is the study, findings and diagnosis?
  • Axial T2FS MRI of the lumbar spine showing L4-5 (need sagittal image for reference to confirm position) demonstrating a focal protrusion (arrow), with base disc herniation wider than AP extension and involving less than 25% disc circumference. 4.11 - Review the following images and provide a diagnosis

76M with longstanding back pain with radicular symptoms with recent exacerbation of pain.
What is the study, findings and diagnosis?
  • The study is a reformatted sagittal CT, bone windows.
  • It is demonstrating loss of disc height, with vacuum phenomenon, minor endplate osteophytosis at L5-S1, and 1.5mm anterolisthesis of L5 on S1.
  • There is mild loss of disc space height with mild disc bulges at L3-4 and L4-5.
  • There is osteoporosis with diffuse decrease in bone density.
  • Note imaging features of Baastrup’s disease: contacting adjacent spinous processes demonstrating cortical irregularity and sclerosis most pronounced at L3-4 (arrow).
  • Diagnoses: Degenerative disc disease, Baastrup’s disease, and osteoporosis

Reference

  • Ref: Chapter 15 Spine O'Neill, JMD, Essential Imaging in Rheumatology. New York: Springer Science+Business Media; 2015

4.12 - Review the following images and provide a diagnosis

57M presenting with bilateral proximal lower limb weakness, skin thickening with discolouration and “hardening” of the distal right leg
What are the imaging modalities, findings, and diagnosis?
  • a) AP radiograph of the right distal tibia with sheet-like subcutaneous calcification (arrows) over the medial margin of the distal tibia
  • b) ultrasound demonstrating calcification as linear hyperechoic bands (arrows)
  • Diagnosis: Provisional diagnosis of dermatomyositis with subcutaneous calcifying panniculitis.
  • This requires further workup and exclusion of underlying malignancy

4.15 - Review the following images and provide a diagnosis

86F presenting with enlarging left shoulder mass and decreased range of motion.
What are the studies, what are the salient findings and what is the most likely diagnosis?
  • a) AP radiograph demonstrates a large soft tissue mass with defined soft tissue margins, and heterogenous internal calcification, originating from the greater tuberosity of the left humerus with significant bone destruction and poorly defined bone margins
  • b) Coronal T2 MRI demonstrates heterogenous high signal intensity in keeping with a chondroid matrix
  • Diagnosis: Chondrosarcoma

Reference

  • Ref: Chapter 17 Bone and Synovial Tumors O'Neill, JMD, Essential Imaging in Rheumatology. New York: Springer Science+Business Media; 2015
