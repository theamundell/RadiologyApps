Review the following images. What is the diagnosis?

Diagnosis: Atlantoaxial Subluxation

  • Lateral cervical spine radiographs in extension (left) and flexion (right) show:
    • There is abnormal widening of the atlantoaxial interval as measured at the base of this articulation in extension (line).
    • Rheumatoid arthritis is the commonest cause with disruption of the transverse ligament due to chronic inflammation (may be asymptomatic, hence radiographs are often performed pre-intubation to assess for stability)
