Case 4.8

52F on longterm treatment for osteoporosis.
What are the studies and what is the likely diagnosis?

Diagnosis

  • a) AP coned radiograph of the left mid- and distal femur demonstrating an incomplete fracture, characterized by subtle irregular lucency of the lateral cortical margin of the femur with associated periosteal reaction (arrow)
  • b) a delayed phase bone scan demonstrates increased uptake at the left femoral fracture site and an unsuspected fracture of the right femur. Fractures are atypical and are in keeping with bisphosphonate related atypical fractures.

Reference

  • Ref: Chapter 11 Metabolic Bone Disease O'Neill, JMD, Essential Imaging in Rheumatology. New York: Springer Science+Business Media; 2015
