Case 4.11

57F with recurrent hemarthrosis and joint pain.
What is the study, what are the salient findings and what is the diagnosis?

Diagnosis

  • MRI Knee a) Axial T2 with marked regions of low signal intensity in the suprapatellar recess (arrows)
  • b) Axial gradient echo sequence exaggerates low signal intensity due to blooming artifact from hemosiderin
  • c) Axial T1 post-gadolinium enhancement demonstrating enhancing soft tissue in the same region
  • Diagnosis: Pigmented Villonodular Synovitis (PVNS)

Reference

  • Ref: Chapter 17 Bone and Synovial Tumors O'Neill, JMD, Essential Imaging in Rheumatology. New York: Springer Science+Business Media; 2015
