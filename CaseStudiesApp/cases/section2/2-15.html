Case 2.15
Review the following case and provide a diagnosis

Clinical Presentation

History
  • 61 year old man
  • History of diabetes mellitus type 2 for 15 years, with retinopathy and nephropathy
  • Swelling of the left ankle, present for several months
  • Mild pain in the ankle present for the past 2 weeks
  • Continues to weight-bear
  • Has had decreased sensation over the feet for the past several years
Physical Examination
  • Appears well
  • Temp 37.8, HR 60, bp 162/90
  • Swelling, warmth, and erythema of left ankle
  • Decreased range of motion of left ankle, limited to 10 degrees of plantar flexion and 10 degrees of dorsiflexion
  • Pitting edema of the foot and ankle to the mid-shin, bilaterally
  • No other swollen or tender joints
  • Diminished sensation to pinprick, light touch, vibration, proprioception bilaterally to knees

Differential Diagnosis - What diagnoses are you considering?

  • The differential for a monoarthritis of the ankle occurring in a patient with diabetes includes:
    • Septic arthritis, osteomyelitis
    • Charcot joint
    • Crystalline arthropathies particularly gout, CPPD
    • Osteoarthritis
    • Less likely peripheral arthritis associated with seronegative spondyloarthropathy, sarcoidosis, etc.

Imaging - What studies would you order?

Radiographs
Radiograph (AP, left ankle)
Describe what you see

Pertinent abnormalities include:

  • Diffuse soft tissue swelling
  • Joint space loss with central erosions
  • Multiple well-corticated avulsed bone fragments
Radiograph (lateral, left ankle)
Describe what you see

Again, Pertinent abnormalities include:

  • Diffuse soft tissue swelling
  • Joint effusion
  • Joint space loss with central erosions
  • Multiple well-corticated avulsed bone fragments
MRI
MRI (ankle) Mid-sagittal, T2, fat-saturated
Describe what you see

Pertinent abnormalities include:

  • Extensive bone marrow edema in tibia, talus, calcaneus
  • Ankle joint effusion communicating with a large collection in Kaeger’s fat pad (arrow)
MRI (ankle) Sagittal, T1, fat-saturated, post-gadolinium
Describe what you see

Pertinent abnormalities include:

  • Diffuse soft tissue and joint enhancement
  • Rim enhancement of the posterior collection
MRI (ankle) Lateral sagittal, T1, fat-saturated, post-gadolinium
Describe what you see

Pertinent abnormalities include:

  • Diffuse soft tissue and joint enhancement
  • Extensive erosions in the fibula (arrow)
  • Tenosynovitis of the adjacent peroneal sheaths with tendinosis
Bone scan
Bone scan (ankle, delayed phase)
Describe what you see

Pertinent abnormalities include:

  • Marked uptake at the left ankle joint on delayed phase imaging

What is the Diagnosis?

The most likely diagnosis is septic arthritis:

  • History of diabetes with complications including evidence of neuropathy, which predisposes to infections of the lower extremity
  • Characteristic findings on radiographs including erosive changes and bony fragmentation
  • Increased uptake on the delayed phase of bone scan
  • Abnormal soft tissue enhancement on MRI overlying the joint suggests infection due to contiguous involvement of the skin
  • MRI also revealed prominent bone marrow edema, and complex joint effusion with intense synovial enhancement
  • Many of these findings can also be seen with a neuropathic arthropathy, but the presence of a periarticular collection strongly suggests infection. Diffusion weighted MRI imaging, not shown, demonstrated restricted diffusion also strongly suggesting infection which was confirmed on aspiration.
