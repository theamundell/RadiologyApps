Case 2.20
Review the following case and provide a diagnosis

Clinical Presentation

History
  • 46 year old woman, active intravenous drug user
  • Had injected into a vein in the left leg 3 days ago
  • Within 24 hours the skin was red and painful, and the entire leg was swollen
  • She developed fevers and presented to the ER
Physical Examination
  • Appears well
  • Temp 36.5, HR 80, bp 124/80
  • Swelling of both wrists, and several MCP’s, PIP’s, and DIP’s
  • Range of motion of both wrists limited to 45 degrees of flexion and 20 degrees of extension
  • Bouchard’s and Heberden’s nodes present on PIP’s and DIP’s
  • No tophi were found
  • Remainder of the examination unremarkable

Differential Diagnosis - What diagnoses are you considering?

  • Most likely, this patient’s findings represent infection, though vascular cause such as deep vein thrombosis or arterial thrombosis should be considered in cases of unilateral leg swelling
  • The infection could represent cellulitis, or could be a deeper infection such as necrotizing fasciitis or pyomyositis

Imaging - What studies would you order?

Radiographs
Radiograph (left leg)
Describe what you see

On this radiograph, note:

  • Soft tissue swelling with heterogeneous density of the soft tissues
  • Note that there is no gas appreciable in the tissue
MRI
  • MRI was performed to rule out necrotizing fasciitis or pyomyositis
MRI left lower leg (Axial STIR)
Describe what you see
  • This MRI image reveals edema of the soft tissues without extension to the muscle layer
  • There is no increased signal within the intermuscular fascial planes although there is significant fluid accumulating at the deep fascia
MRI left lower leg (Axial T1)
Describe what you see
  • This MRI image reveals edema of the soft tissues without extension to the muscle layer
  • There is no loss of signal within the intermuscular fascial planes
MRI lower legs (Coronal STIR)
Describe what you see
  • This MRI image reveals edema of the soft tissues without extension to the muscle layer
  • There is no increased signal within the intermuscular fascial planes

What is the Diagnosis?

The diagnosis is cellulitis, given:

  • Compatible history with extensive soft tissue edema on radiograph and MRI
  • Lack of interfascial fluid tracking/hyperintensity on MRI which would have indicated necrotizing fasciitis
  • Lack of muscle involvement which would have indicated a pyomyositis
