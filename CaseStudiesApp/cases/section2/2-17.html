Case 2.17
Review the following case and provide a diagnosis

Clinical Presentation

History
  • 56 year old man
  • Previous history of back pain lasting several months in his 40’s, self-limiting at that time
  • Now presenting with low back pain with pain radiating down the anterior aspect of the right thigh and into the anteromedial calf x 6 weeks
  • 30 minutes of morning stiffness
  • Difficulty with walking - has had to take time off work
  • No history of trauma
Physical Examination
  • Appears well
  • Temp 36.0, HR 78, bp 146/88
  • Antalgic gait
  • Pain becomes worse with forward flexion, somewhat improved with extension
  • Straight leg raise test positive on the right side
  • Decreased sensation over the anterior thigh and anteromedial calf
  • Decreased knee jerk on the right side
  • 4/5 power of the right ankle dorsiflexors, with 5/5 power otherwise in the muscle groups of the lower extremities

Differential Diagnosis - What diagnoses are you considering?

  • The differential diagnosis for acute back pain with new neurological signs affecting a radicular distribution includes:
    • Degenerative disc disease
      • Facet joint hypertrophy
      • Spondylolysis/spondylolisthesis
    • Disc herniation
    • Acute compression fracture
    • Space-occupying lesion eg tumour, abscess
    • Inflammatory spondyloarthropathy with facet joint involvement

Imaging - What studies would you order?

Radiographs
Radiograph - oblique, lumbar spine
Describe what you see

The pertinent abnormality is:

  • Spondylolysis of the pars intra-articularis of the L4-L5 articulation
MRI
  • On this occasion, an MRI was ordered to assess for nerve root impingement
MRI - sagittal T2, fat-saturated, lumbar spine
Describe what you see

The pertinent abnormalities include:

  • Grade 1 spondylolisthesis of L4 on L5
  • Vertebral end-plate edema
  • Indentation of the thecal sac by the uncovered intervertebral disc
MRI - sagittal T2, fat saturated, lumbar spine (At level of neuroforamina)
Describe what you see

The pertinent abnormalities include:

  • Secondary neuroforaminal stenosis (arrowhead) due to uncovered disc
MRI - axial, T2 fat saturated, lumbar spine
Describe what you see

Pertinent abnormality is:

  • Right-sided neuroforaminal impingement by uncovered disc
  • Impingement of traversing nerve root seen (arrow)

What is the Diagnosis?

  • The cause of this patient’s back pain and L4 radiculopathy is spondylolysis leading to spondylolisthesis with uncovered disc causing neuroforaminal impingement, as seen on MRI.
