Review the case and provide a diagnosis

Case description

  • 60 year old man, on chemotherapy for chronic lymphocytic leukemia
  • History of gout
  • Injury to the area of the lateral malleolus of the left ankle 1 month previous, developed cellulitis and ulceration extending to bone. Recent surgical exploration
  • Worsening symptoms including increasing ankle pain/swelling, fever
  • Further evaluation with MRI requested

Diagnosis

This patient has a soft tissue infection in the ankle with extensive abscess formation, based on the MRI findings as follows:

  • Subcutaneous, extra-articular fluid collection surrounding and abutting the lateral malleolus, consistent with an abscess (intermediate intensity on T1 and high on T2, with peripheral rind of enhancement after gadolinium, and diffusion restriction on DWI imaging i.e. low on ADC and high on EDC)
  • There is a bony sequestrum in the lateral malleolus, rounded focus of bone, high signal on T2, low on T1 without enhancement in keeping with necrotic bone.
  • There is no definite intra-articular extension or synovitis, though given the proximity of the abscess to the joint, the patient is at risk for developing a septic arthritis
