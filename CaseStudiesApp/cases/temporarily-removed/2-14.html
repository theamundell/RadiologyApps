Case 2.14
Review the following case and provide a diagnosis

Clinical Presentation

History
  • You are called to the ER to see a 27F who presents with a 4-month history of lower back pain
  • Her pain is worst in the low back and has progressed to the point she is having difficulty walking
  • Morning stiffness of 2 hour, gelling phenomenon
  • Pain is present with both activity and rest
  • Smokes 1 pack per day
  • History of intravenous drug use
Physical Examination
  • Appears uncomfortable. Temp 36.9 degrees Celsius
  • ++tenderness to palpation of lumbar spine, most prominent on right SI joint
  • Very limited in all movements of the L-spine due to pain
  • FABER + localizing to the right SI joint
  • Normal ROM of the neck

Differential Diagnosis - What diagnoses are you considering?

  • The differential for acute on chronic back pain with mixed mechanical and inflammatory features in a young patient includes:
    • Seronegative spondyloarthropathies
    • Myofascial back pain
    • Trauma
    • Infectious - septic arthritis, osteomyelitis
    • Infiltrative disorders (eg. malignancy) - rare

Imaging - What studies would you order?

Radiographs
Radiograph (AP pelvis)
Describe what you see

The pertinent abnormalities seen on this radiograph are:

  • In comparison to the left SI joint, the right SI joint is less well demarcated, with irregularity at the iliac side
Radiograph (oblique left SI joint)
Describe what you see

Normal SI joint

Radiograph (oblique right SI joint)
Describe what you see

The pertinent abnormalities seen in this radiograph are:

  • Ill-defined joint space with narrowing
  • Joint line irregularity, most prominent at the iliac side
  • Subchondral sclerosis
Leading Diagnosis
  • Septic arthritis must be considered in this scenario, as it requires prompt therapy
  • Alternative diagnoses would be unilateral inflammatory sacroiliitis as seen in psoriatic or reactive arthritis
  • In this case, an MRI was ordered to characterize the joint abnormalities and to plan for further investigation, eg aspirate

Describe what you see

The pertinent abnormalities seen in this MRI are:

  • High signal intensity at both iliac and sacral side of the joint representing bone edema
  • Enhancement of the joint signifying synovitis

What is the Diagnosis?

The most likely diagnosis is: Septic arthritis of the right SI joint

  • Infection of the joint most commonly caused by bacteria, typically Staphylococcus or Streptococcal species
    • Joint Aspirate

    Fluoroscopic-guided aspirate of the SI joint revealed:

    • Total cell count: 126,000 cells/μL
    • Differential: 90% neutrophils
    • Gram positive cocci in pairs and groups seen on gram stain

  • Presentation is typically monoarticular
  • Fever is usually, though not always, present
  • Rapid joint destruction can ensue if untreated, therefore rapid diagnosis and treatment is essential
  • Joint aspirate is essential to confirm diagnosis, identify the organism and determine antibiotic sensitivities
  • Following aspiration, treatment consists of IV antibiotics and joint lavage
