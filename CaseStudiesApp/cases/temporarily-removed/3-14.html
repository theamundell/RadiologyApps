Review the case and provide a diagnosis

Images

Review the following images. What are the pertinent findings, and diagnosis?

Case description

  • 52M with a history of alcohol abuse
  • multiple falls presenting with persistent left ankle and foot pain and swelling for 1 month
  • Exquisite tenderness on examination with temperature difference between feet
  • Radiographs taken 3 weeks earlier were normal.

Diagnosis

The most likely diagnosis is complex regional pain syndrome Type 1, otherwise known as Sudeck’s atrophy, given the following findings:

  • Generalized soft tissue swelling of the entire distal limb
  • Severe generalized osteopenia: subchondral,subperiosteal and intracortical
  • Consistent history with prior injury to the affected limb, swelling, temperature change and diffuse tenderness
